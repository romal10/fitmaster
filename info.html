Our Mission

Our mission is to promote the values of discipline, respect, and perseverance through the practice of Kyokushin Karate. We aim to develop not just skilled martial artists, but also responsible individuals who contribute positively to society.

Our Vision

Our vision is to create a global community of Kyokushin practitioners who inspire one another to achieve their full potential, fostering unity and respect among all martial arts.

Kyokushin Kai Karate

Founded by Masutatsu Oyama

Kyokushin Kai Karate is a style of stand-up, full contact karate founded in 1964 by Masutatsu Oyama. Born on July 27, 1923, in South Korea, Oyama moved to Japan to pursue martial arts. He trained in various styles before developing his own philosophy that emphasizes rigorous training, discipline, and respect.

Oyama gained notoriety for his powerful techniques and strength, famously demonstrating his abilities by breaking bricks and fighting bulls. His dedication led to Kyokushin becoming one of the most influential styles in the world, with millions of practitioners today.

The philosophy of Kyokushin includes a strong emphasis on self-improvement and personal growth, making it not just a sport but a way of life.

Masutatsu Oyama

Kyokushin Today

Today, Kyokushin Kai Karate has spread globally, with dojos in many countries and millions of students. It continues to evolve while retaining the core values set forth by Oyama. Various tournaments and competitions take place annually, promoting the spirit of Kyokushin.
